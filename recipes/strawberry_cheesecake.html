Strawberry Cheesecake

Description

With a graham cracker crust, ultra-creamy filling, and a scrumptious fresh strawberry topping.

Ingredients

Crust

  • 14 graham crackers
  • 1/4 cup of white sugar
  • 6 tablespoons of butter melted

Filling

  • 4 (8 ounces) packages of cream cheese, softened
  • 1 1/4 cups of white sugar
  • 3/4 cup of sour cream, at room temperature
  • 2 teaspoons of vanilla extract
  • 2 teaspoons of fresh lemon juice
  • 1/4 teaspoons of salt
  • 4 large eggs, at room temperature

Strawberry Topping

  • 2 quarts fresh strawberries, stems removed and halved, divided
  • 1/2 cup of white sugar
  • 1 tablespoon of fresh lemon juice
  • 2 teaspoons of vanilla extract
  • 2 teaspoons of cornstarch
  • 1/2 cup of cold water

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 350 Fahrenheit or 175 Degrees Celsius.
  2. Break graham crackers into a bowl of a food processor.
  3. Add sugar, and pulse until large crumbs form. Add butter and pulse just until combined and mixture resembles slightly wet sand.
  4. Lightly grease a 10-inch springform pan; press mixture lightly into a bottom and about 1 inch up the sides of the pan.
  5. Bake in the preheated oven until fragrant and slightly golden brown around the edges, about 8 minutes.
  6. Let cool. Reduce oven temperature to 325 Fahrenheit or 165 Degrees Celsius.
  7. Bring about 3 quarts of water to boil.
  8. PLace cream cheese in the bowl of a stand mixer. Using a paddle attachment, whip cheese on a medium speed just until smooth, to avoid incorporating too much air.
  9. Add sugar and beat until smooth, scraping down the sides of the bowl as needed.
  10. Add in sour cream, vanilla, lemon juice and salt and blend until just combined.
  11. With the mixer on low speed, add eggs, one at a time, mixing until each egg is fully incorporated, then mix until just combined and smooth, scraping down the sides of the bowl as needed.
  12. Pour mixture into a prepared crust. Wrap bottom and sides of the pan well with heavy duty aluminum foil.
  13. Place the pan inside a large roasting pan and set the pan in the oven.
  14. Carefully, pour boiling water into the roasting pan around the cheesecake without letting any water inside the foil or the cheesecake.
  15. Bake in the preheated oven until the center is very slightly jiggly, around 50 minutes.
  16. Carefully, remove pans from the oven and allow cheesecake to cool in the water bath for 15 to 20 minutes.
  17. After about 10 minutes, just when the cheesecake starts to release itself from the sides, run a knife around the sides.
  18. Remove wrapped cheesecake from water bath and place on a wire rack to cool completely, about 1 hour.
  19. Unwrap cheesecake and place in refrigerator until fully chilled and ready to serve, at least 4 hours.
  20. Place 1/2 of strawberries in a large saucepan and stir in sugar, lemon, vanilla, cornstarch, and water.
  21. Bring to a boi over high heat, stirring often. Reduce heat to medium high and allow mixture to simmer until berries after softened.
  22. Using a potato masher, mash strawberries until slightly chunky; continue to simmer until thickened, about 10 minutes.
  23. Remove from heat, stir in remaining strawberries, and let cool to room temperature.
  24. Refrigerate until ready to serve.
  25. Serve cheesecake slices with strawberry topping.
